About the Workshop

The Department of Statistics at the University of Seoul invites you to attend the 4th Bayesian Nonparametrics (BNP) Networking Workshop, to be held at the University of Seoul Centennial Memorial Hall from Monday, July 6 to Friday, July 10, 2026.

The workshop is co-sponsored by the Korean Society of Statistics and the Nonparametric Section of the International Society for Bayesian Analysis (ISBA).

The networking workshop has been held biennially since its inaugural event in 2022 in Cyprus. The BNP Section launched this series to create smaller, informal gatherings with the following goals:

Early-Career Focus

To provide a flexible schedule with dedicated slots for early-career researchers.

Network Building

To establish and strengthen local networks while broadening the appeal of BNP.

Innovation

To foster brainstorming and innovation.

In 2026, the workshop will take place immediately after the ISBA World Meeting in Nagoya, Japan, offering an excellent opportunity to spark engaging discussions and promote deeper networking within the BNP community—particularly valuable for junior researchers.

Location & Venue

Dates

July 6th (Monday), 2026 - July 10th (Friday), 2026

Venue

The conference will be held at the University of Seoul Centennial Memorial Hall, located in the northeastern part of the city. The venue is easily accessible by public transportation and offers modern facilities within the campus of one of Korea's leading public universities.

Travel Information

Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is well connected to major cities around the world:

  • From Europe: Direct flights from London, Paris, and Frankfurt take about 10–12 hours.
  • From Japan: Flights from Tokyo or Nagoya take roughly two hours.
  • From China: Travel times from Beijing and Shanghai are typically two to three hours.
  • From U.S. Cities: Approximately 10–11 hours from Los Angeles, 11 hours from Chicago, and 13 hours from New York.

Accommodation

Seoul is a metropolitan city that offers a wide range of accommodation options. The workshop venue is accessible via Subway Line 1, so areas near Line 1 stations—such as Hoegi, Cheongnyangri, Dongdaemun, Jongno, and Jonggak—are convenient choices. It takes about 30 minutes by subway or bus to reach the workshop venue from Cheongnyangri or Dongdaemun. The campus is also approximately 30–45 minutes from the city center by subway or bus, where many additional hotel options are available.
